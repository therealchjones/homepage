Shaved My Head

2/26/2006 03:17:00 PM

Yup, I did it. Went whole hog. Partly for utilitarian reasons, and partly for my feelings on SuperCuts. Totally bald, probably for the next few days, anyway. Luckily, I'm not alone: there are great role models in shaved heads to emulate.
Kojak
Kojak, quite possibly the most recognizable shaved head of my parent's generation, fighting crime and looking good while doing it.
Lex Luthor
Lex Luthor, quite possibly the most villainous and brilliant shaved head ever. Who else could mastermind a scheme to steal fractions of cents?
Jared Svenning
Mr. Svenning, quite possibly the most recognizable shave head to anyone who went to college in the late 1990's. Mallrats may have been a flop, but it's more than made up for it in geek rentals from the video store.
Jesse Ventura
Jesse Ventura, quite possibly just as fictional as the above shaved heads, and the first cast member of Predator to become governor of one of the United States.
His Holiness the Dalai Lama
The Dalai Lama, the present incarnation of Avalokitesvara, quite possibly the most impressive shaved head ever. Personally, I'd like to attain enlightenment myself—I hear it comes with a pretty nice certificate, suitable for framing.

Want to join this illustrious group? It's not difficult, but it is more so than I'd expected; it definitely helps to have a hand with the back of your head. Don't wish to join, but want to show respect and tolerance? For the sake of politeness, always note how much you like the new look—there's not much the wearer can do about it. Factual notes, like “you shaved your head” aren't really rude, but aren't particularly helpful. Saying “you look like a walking penis” is right out.
