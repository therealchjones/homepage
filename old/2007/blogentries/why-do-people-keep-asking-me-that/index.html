Why do people keep asking me that?

1/25/2006 02:26:00 PM

No, I'm not post-call, and it's not as flattering as you might think to consistently be asked. Sure, it's nice to see that I fit in well on the surgery services, but do I really always seem like I've been up all night?

Admittedly, I'm not typically a bucket of sunshine in the mornings, and I don't shave every day, and there are probably permanent stains on the front of every set of scrubs I own. Still, isn't regularly asking if someone's post-call equivalent to seeing someone just coming from SuperCuts and asking “what happened to your hair?”

Still, this is the worst part of surgery so far, and that's really saying something. I find that I'm really cut out for it, if I can stand being away from home for what seems like days on end. Trauma surgery, the first three weeks of the year, was the best rotation I've had. Really outstanding. Non-trauma, or “Emergency Surgery Service” has been less interesting so far, but it's still surgery, and I still feel like I'm fixing things and doing as well or better than most of my colleagues. It might sound like blasphemy coming from a fan of emergency medicine, but I could seriously imagine myself doing surgery as a career.

So, the question to be answered follows naturally—can I imagine myself doing anything else?
