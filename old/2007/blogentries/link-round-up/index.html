Link Round-up

4/22/2006 09:54:00 AM

No, that's not me, and not a patient I've seen over the last few weeks on my psychiatry rotation, but it is a man who tried to commit suicide with a nailgun, and then reported to an emergency department complaining of a headache. Like far too many patients I've been seeing lately, he was a heavy crystal meth user and had little if any idea what he was doing.

Yup, it's mostly my psychiatry rotation—six weeks in LAC+USC Medical Center's psychiatric emergency room—that's kept me from making a post in over a month. It's been interesting, just a bit time-consuming. There are a few things I've noticed in the last week, however, hence this Round-up. More meaningful commentary and personal stories can wait.

Turns out that there will be some real shake-ups in the next Star Trek movie. It's been a ridiculously long time since I've watched any Trek, but I was still amused to find that the production team from Lost will make the next Star Trek flick. Also turns out this one will be a bit different—it will follow Kirk and Spock as young men in school and on their first mission. Trek meets Lost meets The O.C.? What could possibly go wrong?

Finally, the government is again trying to mandate web self-ratings. Hey, I'm all for putting an “explicit lyrics” tag on this site; it might get rid of the need for that disclaimer I have linked at the top of the page. But why on earth would anyone think that would solve the “problem” of children looking at “inappropriate” material, or especially the real problem of people trying to prey on the young and naive? I'm seriously doubting the possibility of anyone putting a warning on their teeny-bopper site along the lines of “I'm actually a 40-year-old pot-bellied bald man who likes luring prepubescent girls”. Still, maybe that explicit lyrics tag for this site might not be a bad idea….
