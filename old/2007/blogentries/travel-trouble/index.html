Travel Trouble

12/04/2006 12:39:00 PM

As I go 'round the interview trail for residency, I'm slowly becoming accustomed (if not inured) to the headaches of frequent flight in the US. I'm rapidly building up my Continental frequent flier miles, partly because those headaches seemed to be mildly decreased on that particular airline. (For instance, they don't charge extra for meals or movies.)

Still, most of the real annoyances are carrier-independent, and stem from what we've come to call airport security. Newsweek had a nice editorial by Anna Quindlen a few weeks ago entitled “Taking Off Your Shoes” that really hit home and nicely describes the TSA as a prime example of “government run amok”, with incompetence and arbitrariness at its center. Perhaps the conclusion that most rings both true and frightening: “Osama bin Laden could get through the line if the name on his licens was the same as that on his ticket and he wasn't packing Oil of Olay.”

In any case, I haven't had any specific problems with security at the airport, as I check all my liquids (I don't think you can buy 3-ounce bottles of Bed Head), I wear slip-off shoes, and am a lower-middle-class white male. This despite the ridiculous range of electronics that accompany me on the trip. To be honest, I wouldn't mind the privacy issues, either, if I didn't have to wait in line.

Outside security, however, I still don't understand how sitting on a plane for five hours doing nothing productive whatsoever can make me so damn exhausted….
