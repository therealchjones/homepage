Movie Review: The Producers

1/01/2006 08:36:00 PM

Saw the latest musical to make the jump to film today; let me start by saying this is the first time I've seen The Producers; I saw neither the play nor the 1968 film. Why do I mention this? because the review I heard on NPR suggested that it doesn't live up to the legacy. Well, I haven't noted any previous legacy, so according to that review I was still in for a treat.

Maybe it's the habit of watching far too much television, the sickness of seeing mass-produced Hollywood crap at the theater, or just reading too many books that have more happening than could ever be placed into a 2-hour movie, but I've started to notice that musicals are just slow. Rent wasn't too slow, but it wasn't too long, either. The Producers weighs in at a fat 134 minutes, according to IMDb, but easily felt longer. (Oddly enough, on second glance Rent is actually 135 minutes. Amazingly, it didn't seem half as long as this film.)

Minor spoilers (nothing you wouldn't get from the trailer) below.

There were some funny gags. I've often hated Will Ferrell's performances, but this is the best I've ever appreciated him. Outrageous as a former Nazi (or at least a sympathizer), he even makes me want to start re-learning German, if only to make sure I can catch all the jokes in his musical numbers. The musical within the musical, Springtime for Hitler, is pretty funny stuff in and of itself, but we see distressingly little of it.

All in all, it would be a thoroughly average movie, except that it's a musical. Were the music catchy or cool, that might make it better. In this case, it just makes the movie feel like it's dragging—a lot. Is it necessary to sing a song for every emotion? Can't we see actions instead of talking (or singing) about them?

Two stars. Some funny bits, but I found myself looking at—nay, studying—my watch more often than I'd have liked.
