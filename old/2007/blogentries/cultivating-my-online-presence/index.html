Cultivating My Online Presence

6/11/2006 06:04:00 PM

In anticipation of residency applications next fall, and since more prospective employers are looking at online personae, I've decided to start honing my online presence a bit. No, this doesn't mean a politically-correct change to the content of the site, but rather a more strict separation between my “personal” and “professional” sides. I'm open about this, and let it be known that I'm not trying to “hide” my personal side from my future employers or educators, merely that I'm trying to make sure my professional image is the first one they see when taking a look at me.

My disclaimer really already says all I care for it to in terms of my behavior—I do like to have fun when I'm not working, I don't perform actions I consider immoral, and I'm happy to leave it at that. Still, this isn't everyone's cup of tea, and I'm happy for people not to be exposed to it if they're not looking for it too hard. The goal, then, is that from now on people who look for “christianjonesmd” or “ChristianJones.MD” will primarily find my professional persona, and those who search for “chjones” or “TheRealchjones” will find the more personal, hobby-driven side of me. Dig deep enough into either, and you'll find the other—I'm not interested in falling off the landscape and keeping that from happening. Quite frankly, if you're the type of employer who needs to dig deep and not hire me because of something you find that offends you, I'd rather not work for you anyway.

So this will take a bit of work. The first step is the removal of “ChristianJones.MD” from most pages of this site. If it's in the content, like on this page, it'll stay; if it's just in the template, it'll go. Don't worry, the site's not going away—it's just not quite as obviously linked anymore.

The second step is actually building up the “christianjonesmd” brand a bit—searches for that as of this writing actually just bring up a few posts to OpenBSD mailing lists that I inadvertently posted to from a different address. Oh, well; no real harm done.

The third (and fourth, and fifth, etc.) step is to simply participate more online. I've recently discovered Last.fm, a social online music scene, and am kind of digging it at the moment. That, and communities like it, should help build up the “chjones” brand a bit.

Finally, I may at some point in the future stop referring to myself as a “brand”. Not sure—see Thank You For Smoking for more outstanding information on branding. Highly recommended: 4 or 4.5 stars.
