Facebook, MySpace, and Online “Privacy”

9/17/2006 11:55:00 AM

There's been a bit of coverage in both geek and real news lately about privacy at the big “community” sites like Facebook and MySpace. Newsweek even had a cover story a few weeks ago which talked about all the trouble college students have been getting into after posting intimate details (or just pictures of drunken debauchery) online.

I've had a (very) small share of this. This site includes plenty of my daily funny thoughts, and some of those thoughts are offensive to some. Hence the addition of my disclaimer, and, hopefully, a little less interest in how this site is presented to the outside world. Now, I've never actually gotten in any sort of trouble, but I have had superiors express concern that others may not be as “enlightened” as they are.

This leads to two points. First, if you're not willing to deal with the consequences of posting something online, don't do it. It's public. People will see it. Just because it's on a “community” that requires a password doesn't mean you know and are friends with everyone there.

Second, where do we draw the line? Personally, I feel I am willing to deal with the consequences of posting things online. I find them funny, I'm (obviously) not offended by them, and am fairly certain I don't particularly want to work for someone who is. That's my choice—I choose not to censor myself (much) on this site, but to present myself as professionally as possible on ChristianJones.MD. Can someone, even fairly easily, connect the two? Of course. Am I trying to actually separate these parts of my persona? Not at all. However, I am happy to demonstrate that one is “who I am” and one is “how I'll present myself”.

So don't complain that you made the mistake of posting a picture of your latest trip to TJ half-naked and didn't get a job—maybe you shouldn't have posted that. Maybe you wouldn't have fit in well at that job. Guess what—if you do it to yourself, you're not a victim.
