Used CD Trading at lala.com

6/16/2006 06:19:00 PM

I like music. I'm not “into” music, paying attention to upcoming releases and going to concerts regularly, hearing about new indie bands or discovering new stuff just for fun, but I know what I like, and I'm happy listening to it over and over again. I spend far too much time adding metadata to my iTunes tracks, making mixes, and so forth, but have neither the time nor the money to spend on all those things people do when they are really “into” music.

I have no moral objections to downloading music for free from peer-to-peer networks, but it, not too long ago, became increasingly difficult to do so with any sort of assurance I wasn't just downloading noise. iTunes actually has been reasonable, with its 99 cents per track standard, but—believe it or not—there's still a lot of music I haven't been able to find there.

Enter lala.com, the Internet's used CD store. It's actually just a digital infrastructure for trading used CDs between owners; they send you mailers and take $1.50 per trade (from the person receiving the CD), and you build up karma by trading your own away for free. I just got my first two CDs today (Add It Up by Violent Femmes and Third Eye Blind by, well, Third Eye Blind), music I know well but don't mind paying a buck fifty for—for the entire CD, often with liner notes and all.

Join up. Trading CDs is entirely legal, safer on the quality side, and a decent amount of fun in the community.
