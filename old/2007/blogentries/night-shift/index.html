Night Shift

10/11/2005 02:14:00 AM

It's my first night shift on Ob/Gyn, and it's even less eventful than the day shift. Sit around, wait for someone in labor, send them home telling them they're not really in labor, repeat. Yes, really.

Thing is, I really shouldn't be this bored. I have a presentation to do in 12 hours for which I haven't even finished the reading material, I'm sure there's plenty of studying I could be doing (or even—gasp—learning by doing), but I'm just sitting here updating my blog. (Oh, for those of you who bothered to read the last post, yes, this is another update, and I still haven't finished the archives page, but I've decided to say screw it and just not have new posts on the archive page yet.)

So, stream of consciousness it is. It's been more than two years since my last night shift; I'd say that I've lost the feel for it, but I really can't compare. In the ER, I was working constantly. Here, I'm sitting and trying to read. Argh.

In non-medical news, it seems that Apple is going to make some product announcements on Wednesday. Think Secret says they're going to include new iPod, Power Mac, and Powerbook configurations. I've been wanting a Powerbook for a while—who knows, if me likes what me sees on Wednesday, I might just go further into debt. Also, got a pretty good idea of a gift for Lindsey….

Well, that's enough for now. I should be at least pretending to read.
