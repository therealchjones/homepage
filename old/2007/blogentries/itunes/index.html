iTunes “Monthly Subscription Model”

3/09/2006 06:10:00 PM

A few sites are reporting today that the iTunes “Music” Store has made available The Daily Show as a “monthly subscription”, paving the way for moving from $0.99 per song and $1.99 per video to a service more like some others that make you keep paying to play stuff you've already downloaded.

I'm not one to predict what Apple would do in any circumstance. I think the only prediction I've made about the company that's come even partially true is their introduction of Intel-based PowerBooks in January, and that may have just been a wishful-thinking rationalization to buy a PowerBook in November. Still, I'm seeing this less as a “move toward a subscription model” than a simple extension of the already-existing “album” pricing available. The big difference, of course, is that while iTunes will keep track of these upcoming episodes you've paid for, there's nothing to suggest that if you stop paying $9.99 a month you won't be able to watch what you've downloaded.

I'm not sure I'd pay $1.99 for an episode of The Colbert Report anyway, but that's another story.
