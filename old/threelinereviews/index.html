chjones's Three-line Reviews

Three-line reviews are bad. They tell you little (if anything) about the subject being reviewed, and tend (at least in aggregate) to offer more about the subject doing the reviewing. Hardly anyone likes them.

As it happens, I'm an exception. Sure, all of the above is true, but writing a pithy review without using more than 216 characters is an interesting exercise, one that can even be quite difficult if taken seriously. Add the opportunity to quickly skim reviews without delving too much into the text, the nigh complete lack of spoilers due to lack of space, and one can build up a useful catalog of such reviews. (Well, useful to that one, if no others.)

I've tried to do just that on this site. If you're looking for particularly thoughtful or original commentary, please look elsewhere. If you're looking for in-depth analysis of a subject, please do the same. If your goal is to decide at a glance if a movie's worth a $15 ticket or if you should pay for express shipping on that book whose cover is just so pretty, thanks for visiting.

Each review here uses no more than 216 characters, including punctuation and spaces. The punctuation's correct, but the sentences aren't always complete. It's not poetry, it's not prose, it's just a three-line review.
